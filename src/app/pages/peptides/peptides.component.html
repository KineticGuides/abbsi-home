Peptide Therapy Protocol

Peptides are short chains of amino acids that signal your body to regenerate, heal, and optimize function. At ABBSI, we use physician-prescribed peptides to target key health goals with precision and safety.

🔥 Fat Loss & Metabolic Health

Peptides like AOD-9604, Tesofensine, and CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin are used to enhance fat metabolism, stimulate growth hormone, and support weight loss protocols—especially when abdominal fat or insulin resistance are present.

💪 Muscle Gain & Recovery

Peptides such as CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin and BPC-157 promote muscle repair, recovery, and strength. These are ideal for patients focused on performance, longevity, or rehabilitation after injury.

🧠 Mental Focus & Cognitive Health

Peptides like Semax, Selank, and Dihexa help enhance memory, reduce anxiety, and increase mental clarity. These are used in cognitive optimization plans or stress-reduction protocols.

🧬 Longevity & Cellular Repair

Epitalon, FOXO4-DRI, and MOTS-c are used to support mitochondrial function, cellular senescence reversal, and DNA repair—key mechanisms in healthy aging and longevity protocols.

🛡️ Immune Support & Inflammation Control

Thymosin Alpha-1, LL-37, and BPC-157 are used in protocols addressing chronic infections, immune dysregulation, or tissue healing needs.

💗 Sexual Function & Libido

PT-141 (Bremelanotide) and kisspeptin are options for patients seeking improved sexual desire, arousal, or hormonal balance. These peptides are prescribed based on lab data and goals.

✨ Skin, Hair, & Aesthetics

Peptides like GHK-Cu and Melanotan II are used to support collagen production, skin tone, elasticity, and pigmentation. They may be combined with regenerative aesthetic procedures for enhanced results.

📈 Personalized Peptide Plans

Each peptide protocol is guided by lab testing, patient history, and specific goals. Peptides are compounded through trusted pharmacies and administered as prescribed—usually via subcutaneous injection or nasal spray—with ongoing monitoring and adjustment.
